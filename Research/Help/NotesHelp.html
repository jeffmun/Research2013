Recording Notes and Comments

Notes in General

There are several locations on the Autism Center Intranet where written notes can be added. Because the tracking database involves so much information, it can be confusing figuring out where to put specific information.

The first issue is to determine what the information describes. In general, we want the notes tied to the smallest unit to which it applies. For example, if you have a comment to write concerning a particular measure administered to a particular subject, you want to place the note with that subject's measure information rather than place it with the notes about the family as a whole.

The following describes the variety of places where notes can be placed. They are listed from the most specific (or smallest unit) up to the most general.

Measures ("the data")

Any comments related to the administration of a specific measure to a particular subject should be placed in the Notes box on the Measure Status form. You can navigate to this form from a variety of places on the web site.

Actions ("steps we take to get the data")

Sometimes the information applies to a particular study action, such as receiving a questionnaire packet. Although there may be several measures in the packet, if you have comments about the packet as a whole and whether or not we've received it, etc., this should be placed in the Notes box on the Action Status form. You can navigate to this form from a variety of places on the web site.

Subjects

Notes concerning a subject's current level of participation. The Notes section here should be thought of as providing additional information that further describes the subject's "status" and "statusdetail".

Obviously, the decision about where to place notes and the level of detail to report is not always clear. Use your best judgment and be clear.
